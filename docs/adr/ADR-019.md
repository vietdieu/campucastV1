# ADR-019: Recommendation as a Pure Ranker

## Context
In previous iterations, recommendation logic was often buried within pipeline steps or simple feed scoring. To build a highly personalized experience, we need a dedicated engine that ranks content based on multiple intelligence vectors (User, Candidate, Context, and Story).

## Decision
We implement the **Recommendation Engine** as a pure mathematical ranker that operates on enriched data.
1. **The Scoring Formula**:
   `FinalScore = (Σ(InterestVector[i] * IntelligenceVector[i])) * ContextWeight * Authority * Freshness * DiversityPenalty`
2. **Multi-dimensional Inputs**:
   - **User Interests**: Dynamic topic affinity scores from `UserIntelligence`.
   - **Candidate Signals**: Urgency, Importance, Novelty from `CandidateIntelligence`.
   - **User Context**: Morning Commute vs. Work Focus (e.g., preference for shorter/urgent news during commute).
   - **Story Intelligence**: Cluster roles and editorial importance.
3. **Diversity Control**: Implement a sliding penalty for redundant topics or clusters to ensure variety in the top results.
4. **Decoupled Logic**: The engine does not perform its own analysis; it consumes vectors provided by the Intelligence platforms.

## Consequences
- The system can easily tune personalization by adjusting weights in the scoring formula without changing the underlying architecture.
- We can support A/B testing different ranking strategies (e.g., "Freshness-heavy" vs "Interest-heavy").
- The output is a sorted list of `EnrichedCandidate`s, ready for the `AI DJ Composer` to assemble.
