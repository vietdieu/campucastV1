# ADR-007: Immutable Aggregate Rule

## Context
Aggregates in high-scale domain systems should ideally remain immutable once generated. Standardizing this prevents concurrency problems, playback buffer inconsistencies, and data race states when background processes operate on briefings or manifests while clients are actively playing back.

## Decision
We enforce a strict immutability constraint on the `Briefing` aggregate:
1. **Immutable Aggregates**: Once a `Briefing` is generated and saved, it cannot be updated or modified.
2. **Version Promotion (v2, v3)**: If the underlying RSS content or user preferences change after generation, the system MUST generate a *new* separate `Briefing` aggregate (promoting `schemaVersion`, `promptVersion`, or using a new unique `id`) instead of overwriting the existing one.
3. **Cache Coherency**: Audio assets, manifests, and player structures referencing older briefings will continue to stream safely without interruption.

## Consequences
- No race conditions or dirty read issues in client players during active updates.
- 100% stable playback experiences, matching the "Generate Once – Play Many" philosophy.
- Historical briefings are naturally preserved without requiring explicit archiving logic.
