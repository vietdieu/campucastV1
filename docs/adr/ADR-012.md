# ADR-012: Content Intelligence First

## Context
As CommuteCast evolves into a comprehensive Content Intelligence Platform, treating RSS feeds merely as raw transport data (i.e. simple URLs that yield XML) limits our ability to reason about feed quality, reliability, and relevance. If we pipe raw articles directly into LLM generation, we lose control over ranking, deduplication, and intelligent prioritization, while wasting expensive tokens on low-value content.

## Decision
We mandate a "Content Intelligence First" architecture:
1. **No Raw Transport Passthrough**: CommuteCast shall never treat RSS feeds as raw transport data. Every feed must evolve through a measurable intelligence pipeline before entering Briefing Generation.
2. **Candidate Generation**: The RSS Gateway will output `Candidate` objects, not raw articles. A `Candidate` contains the parsed article along with enriched intelligence metadata (e.g., feed score, freshness, confidence, duplicate flags).
3. **Three-Tier Fingerprinting**: Deduplication must happen across three layers:
   - **Transport Layer**: ETag / Last-Modified
   - **Content Layer**: Cryptographic SHA-256 Hash
   - **Semantic Layer**: Conceptual Similarity (AI-driven)
4. **Living Feed Entity**: Sources are modeled as a rich DDD Aggregate (`Feed`) with internal state tracking Health (State Machine), Quality, Fetch Policies, and Statistics.
5. **Strict Governance Rule**: No service after Feed Intelligence is allowed to access RSS XML directly. The `Candidate` object becomes the Single Source of Truth for the entire downstream content layer.

## Consequences
- The Briefing Pipeline (Summarizer) only processes high-quality, scored `Candidate` objects.
- Drastic reduction in LLM API costs and token usage by filtering out duplicates at the transport and content layers.
- Lays the exact groundwork needed for the Sprint #009 Recommendation Engine (AI DJ), which can easily append "User Interest" to the existing Candidate scoring framework.
- Shifts the platform from a dumb aggregator to a smart content curation engine.
