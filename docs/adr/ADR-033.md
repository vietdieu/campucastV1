# ADR-033: Taste Evolves, Preferences Don't

## Context
Traditional recommendation systems often rely on static "User Preferences" (e.g., "User likes Tech"). However, in a radio context, a user's "Taste" is highly situational. What they want to hear during a high-energy morning commute is vastly different from what they seek during a relaxed evening or a weekend long-read session. 

Our current `RecommendationEngine` uses a linear `interestVector`, which is insufficient for capturing the multi-dimensional nature of situational audio consumption.

## Decision
1. **Situational Taste over Static Preference**: We shift from storing "Topic Scores" to storing "Contextual Affinities". A "Taste" is now defined as a combination of `Topic + Context + State`.
2. **Taste Intelligence Platform**: We introduce a unified platform to manage this complexity, consisting of:
   - **Taste Graph**: A multi-dimensional data structure mapping topics to contexts (Time, Weather, Host, Emotion).
   - **Habit Engine**: Detects patterns in listening behavior (e.g., "Always listens to Tech at 7:30 AM").
   - **Context Affinity Engine**: Weights topics based on the current environment (e.g., Weather=Rain -> More "Cozy/Slow" content).
   - **Novelty & Diversity Engines**: Actively prevents "filter bubbles" by enforcing a ratio of familiar vs. surprise content and ensuring topical variety.
3. **No Long-term Cache of "Favorites"**: The system must treat Taste as a living entity that evolves with every skip, completion, and context shift.

## Consequences
- The `AI DJ Orchestrator` (from ADR-032) now has a much deeper understanding of *when* to play *what*.
- We improve "Recommendation Lift" by reducing skips in contexts where certain topics are inappropriate despite high general interest.
- The system architecture moves closer to a true "Editorial AI Radio" that understands the human nuance of time and place.
