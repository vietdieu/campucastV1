# ADR-035: Observe Before Learn

## Status
Proposed (2026-07-04)

## Context
As CommuteCast matures, we need to improve our intelligence layers by learning from past performance. However, naive implementation of "Learning" (e.g., updating taste weights directly based on a skip) introduces high risk of "Intelligence Debt" – where the system incorrectly optimizes for spurious patterns.

## Decision
1. **Separation of Evaluation and Evolution**: We introduce a dedicated `Evaluation Layer` that operates in an observation-only mode. It scores performance without modifying the underlying models (Taste Graph, Recommendation Engine, etc.).
2. **The Observation Pipeline**:
   - `EditorialScorer`: Evaluates narrative and editorial quality (independent of KPIs).
   - `TasteLiftAnalyzer`: Measures the delta in performance (completion rates) between personalized (Taste-driven) and non-personalized sessions.
   - `SessionAnalyzer`: Aggregates holistic metrics (Continuity, Flow, Fatigue).
3. **Learning Queue**: Observations that meet high-confidence thresholds are enqueued for future analysis. *Only after* a validation cycle (to be developed in Sprint #022) can these observations trigger model evolution.

## Rationale
By enforcing an "Observation-Only" constraint during Sprint #021, we ensure that our evaluation metrics (the "Product Score") are stable and meaningful before they are allowed to influence system behavior. This prevents cascading errors and allows us to build trust in our evaluation logic first.

## Consequences
- **Positive**: High-quality data for future learning; system stability preserved.
- **Negative**: Slower "closed-loop" learning initially; requires more infrastructure.
- **Mitigation**: Focus on high-coverage metrics and automated validation to ensure the evaluation engine itself is accurate.
