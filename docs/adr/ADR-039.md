# ADR-039: Knowledge Governance Platform

## Status
Proposed (2026-07-04)

## Context
Following the establishment of ADR-035 (Observe Before Learn), ADR-036 (Learning Happens Offline), ADR-037 (Knowledge Snapshot First), and ADR-038 (Knowledge Registry), we have built a robust separation between real-time execution (Runtime) and offline intelligence updates. However, versioning and storing snapshots are only procedural. To prevent toxic biases, drift, conversational hallucinations, and user experience degradation, we must establish a governing authority over *which* snapshots are allowed to enter the Registry and eventually propagate to the active Runtime.

## Decision
1. **The Knowledge Governance Platform**: We introduce an independent `Knowledge Governance Platform` as the supreme gatekeeper for all learned intelligence. No knowledge snapshot can reach the Knowledge Registry or propagate to the Runtime without completing this governance cycle.
2. **The "No Knowledge Without Governance" Rule**: All candidate snapshots produced by the Offline Learning Platform must pass through five specialized automated and manual governance check gates:
   - **Quality & Performance Alignment Gate**: Ensures the candidate snapshot exceeds baseline Product KPIs (e.g., Editorial Score stability, Taste Lift Accuracy) using historical simulation.
   - **Risk & Guardrails Gate**: Scans editorial adjustments, persona biases, and host dialog configurations to prevent conversational hallucinations, toxic behaviors, or inappropriate emotional arcs.
   - **Audit Trail Gate**: Verifies the snapshot lineage and datasets used during training to establish absolute compliance and explainability.
   - **Canary & Rollout Gate**: Orchestrates progressive canary releases (e.g., serving the new snapshot to 1% of users first) and monitors real-time feedback before wide deployment.
   - **Human/Product Sign-off Gate**: Provides an optional manual dashboard for product managers to review proposed changes, inspect hypothesis generation outputs, and issue cryptographic approvals.

## Rationale
In high-scale personalized applications, allowing models to learn and deploy autonomously creates significant liabilities. By wrapping our registry and learning platform in a strict Governance Layer, we prevent toxic behavioral cascades, ensure regulatory compliance, guarantee that AI behavior remains aligned with brand values, and make the system fully explainable to engineers and business stakeholders.

## Consequences
- **Positive**: Strict quality control, complete mitigation of rogue AI behaviors, bulletproof product safety, compliance readiness.
- **Negative**: Introducing governance gates increases the time-to-production for newly learned parameters.
- **Mitigation**: Optimize automated guardrails to run within minutes, reserving manual sign-off only for major structural snapshots or high-risk hypothesis deployments.
