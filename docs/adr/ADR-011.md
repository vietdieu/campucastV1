# ADR-011: Product Value First

## Context
With the completion of Sprint #004.5, CommuteCast has established an exceptionally robust, highly decoupled, and operationally resilient platform architecture (featuring deterministic processing, step-level telemetry, circuit breakers, dead-letter queues, and deterministic replaying). 

However, engineering-driven platforms often fall into the trap of "Architecture Addiction"—a state where teams continuously layer abstract patterns, micro-events, and frameworks without delivering tangible product improvement. An architectural choice that does not translate into better performance, lower latency, higher relevance, or lower costs for the end-user is a net-negative for the system's overall maintainability.

## Decision
We enforce a strict "Product Value First" mandate for all future development:
1. **Measurable End-User Value**: Every architectural or infrastructure change introduced from Sprint #005 onwards must be justified by a quantitative, measurable improvement to the end-user experience or platform operational metrics.
2. **Standardized Value Mapping**: Engineering tickets and refactoring proposals must map their technical objectives directly to concrete Product KPIs:
   - *Example (Incorrect)*: "Refactor Cache Adapter layers to follow perfect DDD pattern."
   - *Example (Correct)*: "Implement three-tier cache to reduce application cold-startup time from 2.4s to under 0.9s (KPI: Startup Speed)."
3. **Architecture is a Means, Not the End**: Architectural elegance alone is not sufficient justification for code changes. If a system is stable, maintainable, and satisfies current product needs, it must remain frozen.

## Consequences
- Prevents technical over-engineering and keeps the codebase light and performant.
- Drives active alignment between technical complexity and actual business goals.
- Directly supports our Strategic Organizational Metrics ("Measure Less, Learn More") and guarantees a high return on engineering efforts.
- Establishes a professional, product-focused software development culture similar to modern high-performance teams at Spotify, Discord, and Linear.
