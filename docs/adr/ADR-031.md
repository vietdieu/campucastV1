# ADR-031: Broadcast Before Performance

## Context
Following the completion of the `Persona Platform` (ADR-030), we have defined the "Editorial Identity" (who is speaking and how they speak). However, before synthesizing audio, we need an "Executive Producer" to decide the flow of the show.

Historically, TTS engines and audio synthesis have been tightly coupled with content generation. This leads to rigid, robotic broadcasts where stories are just concatenated together. A real radio show relies on structured pacing, tempo shifts, energy curves, and logical sections (e.g., Opening, Lead, Deep Dive, Break, Reflection). 

## Decision
1. **Broadcast Before Performance**: We introduce the `Broadcast Director` as an intermediate layer between the `Persona Platform` and the `Performance Director`. The Broadcast Director is strictly responsible for *show structure* and *pacing*, without generating content or audio.
2. **The Broadcast Plan**: The Director will output a `BroadcastPlan` DTO, which includes:
   - `sections`: An ordered list of broadcast segments (e.g., Opening, LeadStory, MusicBreak).
   - `tempoCurve`: The pacing for each section (Fast, Medium, Slow).
   - `energyCurve`: The emotional energy for each section (High, Peak, Reflection, Warm).
3. **Dedicated Sub-Engines**:
   - `BroadcastPolicy`: Enforces constraints based on the user's context (e.g., morning commute <= 12 minutes, no deep dives while driving).
   - `SectionPlanner`: Determines the sequence of show segments.
   - `TempoEngine`: Assigns appropriate tempo to each section based on context.
   - `EnergyCurveEngine`: Designs the high/medium/reflection energy curve for the show.

## Consequences
- The architecture correctly mirrors a real-world radio station: The `Editorial Brain` (Writer) -> `Persona Platform` (Host) -> `Broadcast Director` (Producer) -> `Performance Director` (Audio Engineer).
- We achieve emotional continuity and tempo stability throughout the broadcast, preventing abrupt shifts from "Emergency" to "Funny".
- The `Performance Director` is greatly simplified, as it only needs to execute the `BroadcastPlan` and render the final audio.
