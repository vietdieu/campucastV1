# ADR-032: Session Before Segment

## Context
Following the completion of the `Broadcast Director` (ADR-031), we have all the individual "engineers" and "producers" in place. However, a radio experience is not just a sequence of stories; it is a continuous journey. Currently, our system treats each story and each broadcast section as a somewhat isolated unit.

To reach the next level of user experience, we need to transition from "Segment-Oriented" logic to "Session-Oriented" orchestration. The user doesn't just listen to a "Lead Story"; they listen to a "Morning Commute Show".

## Decision
1. **The Listening Session as Primary Aggregate**: We introduce the `ListeningSession` as the root aggregate of the broadcast. It encapsulates the User Context, Broadcast Plan, Persona, and the actual Timeline of execution.
2. **AI DJ Orchestrator (The Conductor)**: We introduce an Orchestrator layer that acts as the single point of coordination. It doesn't replace the Director or the Persona Platform; instead, it *conducts* them to fulfill specific **Session Objectives** (e.g., "Inform Quickly", "Calm Ending").
3. **Dynamic Adaptation**: The session must be capable of adapting in real-time. If a user skips a story or if their arrival time changes, the AI DJ must recalculate the remaining timeline, not just proceed blindly.
4. **Holistic Metrics**: Success is measured by `Session Completion` and `Experience Continuity`, rather than just the relevance of individual stories.

## Consequences
- The system becomes a true "AI Radio" platform rather than a smart news aggregator.
- Logic for handling user interrupts (skips, pauses) moves from the Runtime into the Domain (AI DJ Orchestrator).
- We create a foundation for the `Taste Graph` (Sprint #019), which will feed into session-level personalization.
