# ADR-029: Editorial Planner & Taste Graph

## Context
Following the shift towards "Editorial Intelligence First" (ADR-028), we recognized that simply moving from Recommendation to Persona and Narrative lacked an executive pacing mechanism. A Persona knows *how* to speak, and a Broadcast Director knows *when* to speak, but there was a gap regarding the overall session strategy: identifying session goals, story depth, and overall structure *before* the stories are actually shaped.

Furthermore, a truly personalized AI Radio Host must have an evolving understanding of context (time of day, commute length, specific persona affinity) rather than just topic affinities. 

## Decision
1. **Introduce the Editorial Planner**:
   - We will insert an `Editorial Planner` layer between the `Recommendation Engine` and the `Editorial Brain`.
   - The `Editorial Planner` will act as the "Producer", responsible for:
     - Determining the session goals and constraints (e.g., duration, number of stories).
     - Deciding the depth of each story.
     - Deciding pacing, transitions, when to recap, and when to end the broadcast.
2. **Enhance the Editorial Brain**:
   - We are introducing the `Editorial Intent Engine` within the `Editorial Brain` (alongside Curiosity, Emotion Arc, Narrative Arc, Surprise, and Memory Recall).
   - This engine will apply intentional framing to stories (e.g., framing a story as "Breaking news" vs. "An interesting development").
3. **Elevate Personalization to the Taste Graph**:
   - The user profiling system will transcend basic topic counting. It will map a multidimensional `Taste Graph`.
   - The Taste Graph will correlate: User + Topic + Time + Commute Length + Host + Emotion + Weather + Day of week.
4. **Implement Long-term Companion Memory**:
   - The `Memory Recall Engine` will expand beyond immediate session context into a long-term Memory layer. It will track recurring interests, personal milestones, story follow-ups, and learning preferences, forming the foundation of a true Conversational AI Host.

## Consequences
- The Recommendation Engine is now freed from attempting to construct the full broadcast structure; it simply ranks and recommends content candidates.
- The Editorial Planner takes over the strategic assembly of the broadcast.
- The Broadcast Director is significantly simplified, acting only on the plan defined by the Editorial Planner.
- Personalization jumps from generic news feed algorithms to Spotify-like AI DJ recommendations based on multi-dimensional listening contexts.
