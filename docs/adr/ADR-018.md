# ADR-018: Story Intelligence as the Narrative Backbone

## Context
A simple recommendation engine sorts items by a single score. However, a radio-like experience (AI DJ) requires narrative flow. If we recommend three separate "Apple AI" articles in a row without knowing they are related, the experience is repetitive. If we don't know which story is the "Lead" (anchor) and which is "Supporting", the transitions feel robotic.

## Decision
We implement a **Story Intelligence Platform** between Candidate Intelligence and Recommendation.
1. **Story Clustering**: Candidates are grouped into `StoryClusters` based on semantic similarity (shared entities, topics, and temporal proximity).
2. **Editorial Role Assignment**: Within a cluster, each candidate is assigned a `StoryRole`:
    - **Lead**: The primary anchor story with the highest authority or urgency.
    - **Supporting**: Contextual stories that add depth to the Lead.
    - **Transition**: Stories that bridge two different clusters.
    - **Closing**: A summary or "kicker" story to end a segment.
3. **Relationship Mapping**: Define relationships like `Continuation`, `Opposition`, or `Expansion` between stories.

## Consequences
- The Recommendation Engine can now rank *clusters* instead of individual articles, or prioritize `Lead` stories while keeping `Supporting` stories as options for "Deep Dives".
- The AI DJ Composer gains a logical blueprint for assembling a narrative (e.g., Lead -> Transition -> Lead).
- Reduces content redundancy significantly.
